# Competitive Analysis: Sintra.ai vs. Raava

**Prepared for:** CEO (investor response)
**Date:** April 3, 2026
**Produced by:** Startup Advisor (Kai), VP Product (Diana)

---

## What Sintra Does

Sintra.ai is a Vilnius-based AI productivity platform ($17M seed, Earlybird VC-led, ~40,000 paying customers, ~$10.5M ARR) that offers 12 pre-built "AI helpers" -- each a chat-based persona specialized in a business function (SEO, email marketing, social media, customer support, etc.). Users interact with helpers through a chat interface, powered by a "Brain AI" knowledge base that stores company context. It positions as "AI employees" but functionally operates as a multi-persona chatbot with basic integrations to Gmail, Google Calendar, Notion, LinkedIn, and Instagram. Priced at $39-97/month with a 250-credit monthly cap across all plans. Uses GPT-4.1 and Claude models under the hood, auto-routed by task type.

## What Raava Does Differently (The Pitch)

Sintra gives you 12 chatbots that generate drafts you copy-paste into your real tools. Raava gives you actual team members that log into your tools, do the work, and report back. Sintra's helpers can't talk to each other, can't build workflows, and can't take action outside of chat. Raava's team members operate in dedicated infrastructure with real credential access, persistent memory, org hierarchy, and multi-agent coordination -- from solo tasks up through full organizational swarms. The difference is the gap between a suggestion engine and an employee.

## 3 Key Differentiators the CEO Should Tell the Investor

**1. "Sintra generates drafts. Raava completes tasks."**
Sintra's helpers live inside a chat window. They write copy, suggest ideas, and generate text -- but the user must copy-paste that output into Gmail, HubSpot, or wherever it needs to go. Raava's team members have real credentials, connect to real tools, and execute autonomously. The user assigns a task; the team member does it. This is the difference between an AI that writes a follow-up email draft and an AI that actually sends the follow-up email.

**2. "Sintra's agents can't talk to each other. Ours are an organization."**
Sintra's 12 helpers are siloed -- they don't share context, can't coordinate, and can't hand off work. If you use their SEO helper and then switch to their email helper, you start from scratch. Raava's 4-tier architecture (Solo, Pod, Swarm, Org) means agents coordinate, share context, escalate, and work as a structured team. This is what "AI workforce" actually means -- not 12 separate chatbots wearing different hats.

**3. "We built for the operator. They built for the content creator."**
Sintra's sweet spot is a solopreneur who needs help writing social media posts and blog drafts. That's useful but narrow. Raava is built for the Head of Ops at a 30-person company who needs to automate CRM follow-ups, run recurring reports, manage support tickets, and coordinate across functions. Different customer, different TAM, different revenue ceiling.

---

## 1 Honest Weakness

**Sintra has 40,000 paying customers and $10.5M ARR. We have zero.**

Sintra has market validation, user feedback loops, and revenue. They've proven demand exists for AI business helpers at the SMB level. Their 4.5-star Trustpilot rating across 8,200+ reviews means real people find real value. They also have a meaningfully lower entry price ($39/mo vs. our $99/mo floor) which reduces friction for first-time AI buyers. Their gamified onboarding is polished and approachable for non-technical users.

The investor should know: we believe our architecture is categorically stronger, but Sintra has the distribution advantage of being live and growing. Our job between now and eMerge is to prove that the deeper product justifies the higher price point -- that operators will pay more for agents that actually do the work versus agents that suggest the work.
